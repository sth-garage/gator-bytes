Job Summary & Key Responsibilities

Role Overview
Serve as the instructional and curricular leader for the Mathematics Department while teaching advanced-level courses, with a primary focus on AP Calculus AB/BC and other upper-level mathematics.

We are seeking a passionate and experienced educator to serve as the Head of the Mathematics Department. In this leadership role, you will oversee curriculum development, mentor fellow faculty members, and champion excellence in teaching. In addition to departmental responsibilities, you will teach higher-level courses.

Key Responsibilities

  • Provide instructional leadership for the mathematics department (grades 9–12).
  • Supervise, mentor, and evaluate a team of 6 math teachers.
  • Develop, implement, and periodically review the math curriculum to ensure alignment with state standards and college readiness benchmarks.
  • Teach 2–3 sections of upper-level mathematics, including AP Calculus AB/BC and/or Pre-Calculus.
  • Monitor and analyze student data to improve instructional practices and student outcomes.
  • Coordinate AP math course offerings, summer bridge programs, and math competitions.
  • Lead department meetings and facilitate professional development workshops.
  • Collaborate with school administration to develop department goals and initiatives.
  • Communicate regularly with students, parents, and guardians regarding course content and student progress.
  • Foster a culture of enthusiasm for mathematics among students and staff.

Qualifications

Required & Preferred
  • Bachelor’s degree in mathematics, mathematics education, or a related field; Master’s degree preferred.
  • Valid State of Illinois Professional Educator License with secondary mathematics endorsement.
  • Minimum 5 years’ teaching experience in high school mathematics, including AP Calculus.
  • Demonstrated leadership experience (department chair, curriculum coordinator, or similar position).
  • Strong understanding of best practices in math instruction and curriculum development.
  • Commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion in education.
  • Excellent communication, organizational, and interpersonal skills.
  • Familiarity with technology integration in mathematics instruction.
